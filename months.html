Interesting Facts by Month

January

Position: First month of the year.

Fun Fact: Named after Janus, the Roman god of doors and beginnings.

February

Position: Second month of the year.

Fun Fact: Named after Februa, a Roman festival of purification.

March

Position: Third month of the year.

Fun Fact: Named after Mars, the Roman god of war.

April

Position: Fourth month of the year.

Fun Fact: Possibly derived from the Latin word "aperire," meaning "to open," referring to flowers blooming.

May

Position: Fifth month of the year.

Fun Fact: Named after Maia, an earth goddess of growing plants.

June

Position: Sixth month of the year.

Fun Fact: Named after Juno, the Roman goddess of marriage and childbirth.

July

Position: Seventh month of the year.

Fun Fact: Originally called Quintilis, renamed in honor of Julius Caesar.

August

Position: Eighth month of the year.

Fun Fact: Originally Sextilis, renamed for Augustus Caesar.

September

Position: Ninth month of the year.

Fun Fact: From Latin "septem," meaning seven (it was the seventh month in the Roman calendar).

October

Position: Tenth month of the year.

Fun Fact: From Latin "octo," meaning eight (it was the eighth month in the Roman calendar).

November

Position: Eleventh month of the year.

Fun Fact: From Latin "novem," meaning nine (it was the ninth month in the Roman calendar).

December

Position: Twelfth month of the year.

Fun Fact: From Latin "decem," meaning ten (it was the tenth month in the Roman calendar).
